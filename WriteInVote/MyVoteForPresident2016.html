My vote for president 2016 - it didn't quite go as planned.

In an earlier article, I explained why I couldn't vote for either Hillary Clinton or Donald Trump. Instead, I planned to write in Ben Carson. The following is the story of my voting experience in 2016. For those that think the system is rigged, you might want to read this - at least for me, it was!

10/22/2016

I decided I was going to vote for Ben Carson and elected to write an article explaining why.

10/23/2016

Printed out 50 copies of the article, hand addressed the envelops (I had previously identified television and radio stations in the state of Florida), folded and stuffed all 50 envelops, and then stamped and sealed the envelops.

10/24/2016

On the way to vote, I was listening to the radio (105.9 Sunny FM) and by chance, heard that there was a Florida law which prohibited taking a picture of your ballot (it is known as the "selfie ballot ban"). I was shocked by this because I had heard on the news that a federal court had already ruled that the ban was unconstitutional. But apparently not all the states have gotten the message. This was extremely disappointing to me because I had my camera packed in the car and was ready to vote for Ben Carson. I had considered merely trying to take a picture anyway, but I figured it would be really hard to sneak my bulky camera into the voting station (after all, I'm one of the few that still uses a flip phone).

So I still voted for Ben Carson, but did not get a chance to take a picture. I asked an election official and they said I definitely was not allowed to take a picture. I also asked if I could get a receipt for my ballot - but sadly, they said no to that as well.

10/24/2016

I had to cut open all 50 sealed envelops and include a cover letter warning about the selfie ballot ban. Then I had to reseal all 50 envelops with tape. It looked pretty cheap, but I wasn't about to spend money on another 50 stamps, and I certainly didn't want to re-address 50 envelops. But at least I didn't send out an article encouraging people to break the law - that would have been horrible.

10/26/2016

Filed a complaint with Florida Attorney General Pam Bondi.

I wrote ...

    I think the "no selfie" law (and $1000 dollar fine) regarding taking a photo of your ballot is not only unfair, but probably is unconstitutional. The premise behind a secret ballot is to protect individual voters from intimidation and the like, it is NOT to suppress public debate. And it is completely contrary to our democratic system to allow for disproportionate influence when you consider the fact that other states do allow photos of ballots (so the consequence of the Florida law is to put citizens of Florida at a disadvantage).

    For me personally, I voted yesterday, and due to Florida law which will not even allow my vote to count (because I wrote in someone else for president), and coupled with the Florida "no selfie" law, my vote for president was completely voided. This is NOT the case in other states, and I am left with little (or no) recourse. So I'm writing to you to ask for immediate resolution. This isn't merely an instance of me blowing off steam - this is a real case of voter suppression, and I believe that I have legal standing. I don't like using the court system for trivial cases, but when the media and those in power have a disproportionate influence on the outcome of an election, something has to be done. So please respond to this email ASAP (within 24 hours).

    From my perspective, the only recourse that I think would be fair is to:

    • temporarily relax the selfie law in Florida (with some controls to ensure the integrity of the secret ballot)

    • allow me to mock up a real election ballot so that I can take a picture of it (that mocked up ballot would obviously have to be destroyed, so this would have to be supervised by an election official).

    I spoke to an election official when I voted yesterday, and he said that I could mock up a sample ballot and post that online. But my point is the power to influence isn't based on a mock up - it is based on an actual ballot. Now if the state of Florida offered a "receipt" for my ballot, then I would be fine with that - but when I asked an election official for a receipt, they said that wasn't possible. So from my perspective, it is kind of like the election never happened for me - and that is extremely unfair.

    Anyway, if I don't hear back soon, I'm going to have to try something more than just an email. BTW, I'm not a lawyer, so I have no idea regarding the process to dispute this (i.e., assuming you tell me that I'm out of luck), so if you have some "hints" on what my next course of action would be, that would be appreciated. I'm not looking to make a stink about this, I just want things to be fair. And obviously, if you wait too long on this, the harm will be irreversible because the election will be over.

    Thanks in advance for your consideration.

10/26/2016

Forwarded a copy of the complaint to my U.S. Congressional Representative (John Mica).

10/26/2016

John Mica's office forwarded the complaint to Florida state representative Bob Cortes.

10/26/2016
